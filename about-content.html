About the Old World Atlas

The Atlas of the Old World is a fan project dedicated to mapping the world of Warhammer Fantasy. This project attempts to create, as much as is possible, an accurate and consistent representation of the Warhammer world, with gaps in the lore generously filled with my own interpretations or the interpretations of others in the community. No AI is used in any part of the creative process; topographic maps, rivers, settlement names and positions are all hand-drawn/-written/-placed. The front-end Javascript used for displaying the project in the form of a webpage was assisted in part by AI.

Please note that there are conflicting and inconsistent sources for the locations and populations of many sites in the Warhammer world. The following 'canonicity tier list' was approximately used to prioritize sources when discrepancies arose. This is a personal choice and may differ from other interpretations:
  1. Recent Games Workshop publications (Warhammer Fantasy Roleplay 4e, Warhammer:The Old World material)
  2. Maps and gazetteers by Andy Law, both published (WFRP 2e) and unpublished, leaning on the extraordinary work of Andy and Lindsay Law in collating decades of lore references for their maps
  3. Older Games Workshop publications (WFRP 1e, WFB army books pre-8th edition)
  4. Other fan interpretations and resources (MadAlfred, community maps, etc.)

Future Features

This version of the project is in a very early stage, with many updates and planned features to come:

  • Improved usability and readability on mobile devices.
  • Expand the map to include Kislev, Norsca, Tilea, Estalia, the Border Princes, Albion, and the Karaz Ankor with equally detailed accuracy.
  • Additional layers including national, provincial, and regional borders; forest cover; icons to signify e.g. ports, capitals, and other points of interest.
  • Depiction of the borders and political situation in the Empire at three points in history:
    • 2519 IC (The End Times)
    • 2512 IC (The Enemy Within campaign)
    • 2276 IC (The Old World)
  • Functionality for user-selectable fonts, color palettes, and other visual customizations, and the ability to export print-quality images.
  • Route-finding algorithm to automatically generate distances, durations, and dangers for travel routes between any two locations.
  • Expand and improve the gazetteers page.
